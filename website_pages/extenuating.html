Extenuating circumstances
What do I do if I have problems which are affecting my ability to study?
Don’t struggle with these problems on your own! We are here to help you to find ways to resolve your problems but you need to ensure you contact us as soon as you possibly can.

If you need help, contact one of the following - who you choose may depend on the nature of the problem:

1. Personal Tutor
2. MLA College's Student Experience team (email: Student Experience)
3. Programme Lead, Dr Marie Hale (email: Dr Marie Hale)
If the problems are severe and affecting your ability to meet an assessment submission deadline, complete and submit an extenuating circumstances form as soon as you are able to. You can download the form by clicking the icon below. We recommend that you read the two supporting documents before you fill in your application. If you have an internet connection, Plymouth University provide some further supporting information here.

Policy and procedures
Regulations
In completing an extenuating circumstances form, please remember that:

1) It is essential that you provide the module code and submission deadline details correctly.
2) Independent corroborative evidence is required.
3) The Universityâ€™s revised regulations for ECs now permit one self-certification of illness in each academic year, the details of which can be read in the attached documents (NB: this still requires a formal EC application to be made).
4) The date of the start and end of the problem must be clearly stated and confirmed by the corroborative evidence provided.
5) The form must be completed at the time of the problem and submitted before, or as soon as possible after a missed assessment deadline; not many months later.
6) The Extenuating Circumstances claim form and corroborative evidence should be submitted electronically to student.experience@mla.ac.uk or to your module tutor (you can find their contact details here).
7) You must hand in the missed assessment as soon as possible and not wait for a decision on your Extenuating Circumstances claim.
8) Extensions are not limitless. Only one extension may be granted per assessment. If an extension is approved, a maximum of 28 calendar days from the original deadline (part-time students)/five calander days from the original deadline (full-time students) will be permitted for the submission of work.
9) You will be advised of the extended deadline by email sent to your university computing account.

Whoever is helping you with your problem may also be able to assist you with completing the form.

If you have a personal problem which you have not discussed with your Programme Leader/Personal Tutor it would be sensible to alert them that there is a problem, even if you do not wish to give much detail.

What happens next?

All decisions and supporting documentation will be formally recorded by MLA’s Student Experience Team in accordance with the procedure outlined in the document above. The Student Experience Team will then communicate the decision to you, your tutor/supervisor, and our Academic Partners.

 

If you have any concerns or would like any further information please do not hesitate to contact us - we are here to help and your welfare is important to us.
